Who is impacted by sensory design?

Many of us, whether or not we have sensory differences, have experienced overstimulation or ‘sensory overload’ in some form. We have all experienced moments where thoughtful sensory design would have had a positive impact on our personal comfort.

Sensitivity to sensory stimuli is not the only symptom people with autism experience, but it is the one with arguably the largest impact on quality of life.

Sensory cues not only determine how we experience the world, but also how we see ourselves as a piece within it.

Autism and Community

Overview

Autism spectrum disorder is a broad term used to describe a condition which may present itself with varying degrees of severity

Common differences experienced by those who receive a diagnosis include discomfort engaging in social interaction, repetitive behaviors and interests, and different ways of learning and paying attention. Symptoms experienced by individuals with ASD vary greatly, but central characteristic is increased sensitivity to sensory stimulation, including but not limited to light, sound, touch, and physical comfort

Stats

No individual life can or should be equated to a simple dollar value; yet, we cannot ignore the special needs of individuals with ASD, and should recognize shared public responsibility in meeting them

1 in 54 youth are diagnosed with ASD in the United States (2016)

ASD diagnoses have tripled since 2000, and are expected to continue rising. 1 in 4 individuals diagnosed with ASD are female

$840,000 is the added cost, on average, to ensure the needs of a youth with ASD are met until age 18 compared to a neurotypical child
